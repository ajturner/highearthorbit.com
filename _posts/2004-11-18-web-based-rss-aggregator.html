So I just installed Feed on Feeds, which is a web-based RSS aggregator. I am frequently moving between computers, both at my house, and while traveling, etc. Therefore, having a single, web-based aggregator is a pretty nice deal. At least, a good solution for the moment.

This is very similar to Feedster, except that I can put it on my own server, and also not be a-feared as to what some ad-tracker thinks of my viewing habits and attempts to put me in a box (or small cage with bars).

A better solution would be able to sync together all my subscriptions, bookmarks, where I've read, etc. with my various aggregators.

Too much information, my brain huuuurts....
